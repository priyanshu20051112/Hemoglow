Some Interesting Facts:
  • World Blood Donor Day is celebrated on June 14th. This day honors Karl Landsteiner, the scientist who discovered the ABO blood group system. It's a day to thank volunteer blood donors for their life-saving gifts.
  • Donating blood is a mini health check-up. Before you donate, you receive a free mini-physical that checks your pulse, blood pressure, temperature, and iron levels. This can sometimes alert donors to potential health issues they were unaware of.
  • A single donation can save up to three lives. After a whole blood donation is collected, it is separated into its components: red blood cells, plasma, and platelets. Each of these components can be used to treat different patients with specific needs.
